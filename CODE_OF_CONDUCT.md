# Code of Conduct - Eclexia Project

## Compassionate Collaborative Culture Protocol (CCCP)

This project follows the **Compassionate Collaborative Culture Protocol (CCCP)** with emotional safety monitoring and restorative practices.

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free, psychologically safe, and enriching experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, political affiliation, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, compassionate, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors (Encouraged)

- **Compassionate Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Empathetic Engagement**: Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Constructive Feedback**: Gracefully accepting and providing constructive criticism
- **Accountability**: Focusing on what is best for the community and accepting responsibility
- **Kindness**: Showing empathy and kindness toward other people
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Prioritizing community benefit over individual recognition
- **Growth Mindset**: Embracing learning opportunities and helping others learn
- **Psychological Safety**: Creating space where people feel safe to contribute and take risks

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable:

- **Violence & Threats**: The use of violent or threatening language or behavior
- **Discriminatory Language**: Derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Harassment**: Public or private harassment of any kind
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Sexual Misconduct**: Unwelcome sexual attention, advances, or imagery
- **Trolling**: Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- **Disruption**: Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- **Boundary Violations**: Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Emotional Safety Monitoring

### Well-being Check-ins

Contributors experiencing emotional distress are encouraged to:

1. **Self-Care First**: Take breaks when needed without explanation
2. **Request Support**: Reach out to project maintainers privately
3. **Set Boundaries**: Communicate capacity limits without guilt
4. **Seek Resources**: Access mental health resources (see below)

### Burnout Prevention

- **Sustainable Pace**: Contributions are valued regardless of frequency
- **No Obligation**: There is never an obligation to respond immediately
- **Workload Balance**: Maintainers monitor contributor workload for signs of burnout
- **Appreciation**: Regular recognition of all contributions, large and small

### Mental Health Resources

Contributors struggling with mental health challenges:

- **Crisis Support**: [International Mental Health Hotlines](https://www.opencounseling.com/suicide-hotlines)
- **Professional Help**: Seek licensed mental health professionals
- **Community**: Join support groups for open source contributors
- **Project Support**: Contact maintainers for accommodations

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Restorative Justice Approach

Rather than purely punitive measures, we emphasize:

1. **Understanding**: Seek to understand the context and motivations
2. **Dialogue**: Facilitate conversations between affected parties
3. **Repair**: Focus on repairing harm and rebuilding relationships
4. **Growth**: Support learning and behavioral change
5. **Accountability**: Ensure genuine accountability for impact

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Project communication channels (chat, email, forums)
- Official social media accounts
- Community events (virtual or in-person)
- Representing the project in public spaces

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project team at:

- **Email**: conduct@eclexia.dev
- **Private Message**: Contact project maintainers directly
- **Anonymous**: Use anonymous reporting form (if available)

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, with full confidentiality.

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Initial Assessment**: Within 3 days
- **Resolution**: Within 14 days (complex cases may take longer)
- **Follow-up**: Check-in with affected parties after 30 days

### Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Minor inappropriate language or behavior.

**Consequence**: Private, written warning with clarity around the violation and explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: Temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: Permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

### Appeals Process

Decisions may be appealed by:

1. **Submitting Appeal**: Email conduct@eclexia.dev with "Appeal" in subject
2. **Provide Context**: Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. **Review**: Appeals reviewed by different maintainer(s)
4. **Decision**: Final decision communicated within 14 days

## Confidentiality

All reports and communications will be kept confidential. Information will only be shared:

- With project maintainers involved in enforcement
- As required by law
- With explicit permission from affected parties
